NASA launched the Kepler space telescope in 2009. It targeted nearly 200,000 stars in the Milky Way in summer, followed by 300,000 in the sky. Some stars have planets, and some planets pass directly in front of their stars. We determine the size and age of these planetary systems by measuring the properties of stars. Kepler has stopped collecting new observations, but TESS launched in April 2018 to survey almost the entire sky. We have a lot of work to do!

  • Our story

    Our story began in 2019 when astronomer Tianchuan Yu, with the help of the government, co-created Froncheer with Dr.Qing Zhu .

  • Discover habitable planets

    With the help of Kepler, we examined all possible worlds around the Earth where life could exist, and crucially, liquid water!

  • A black hole

    The existence of black holes in theoretical physics was clearly observed last year, making us pioneers in the study of black holes.

  • Manned space flight

    We've joined up with a number of national space agencies to provide commercial spacecraft to launch missions to Mars next year.
